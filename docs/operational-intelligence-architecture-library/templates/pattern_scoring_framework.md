# Pattern Scoring Framework

Use this framework to evaluate whether a discovered pattern is worth keeping, prototyping, or promoting into canon.

Score each category from 1 to 5.

## 1. Operational relevance
Does this solve a real workflow problem?

## 2. Boundary clarity
Are the roles, layers, and limits explicit?

## 3. Deterministic compatibility
Can this pattern coexist with strong validation and controlled execution?

## 4. Failure resilience
Does the pattern account for error handling, ambiguity, or recovery?

## 5. Observability
Can the pattern be inspected, traced, and audited?

## 6. Human collaboration fit
Does it support clean escalation, review, or operator control when needed?

## 7. Reusability
Can the principle be applied across multiple systems or workflows?

## 8. Implementation realism
Can this actually be built and maintained without heroic complexity?

## 9. Product fit
How strong is the fit for:
- Clinic Operations OS
- AIRYS
- future operational AI runtimes

## 10. Strategic leverage
Would adopting this pattern meaningfully improve your architecture advantage?

## Score interpretation

### 42–50
Strong canon candidate.

### 32–41
Valuable pattern. Keep and prototype selectively.

### 22–31
Interesting but limited. Keep for inspiration or narrow use.

### 10–21
Weak fit. Do not promote.

## Extra rule

A high score should not overrule a critical flaw in:
- safety
- operational trust
- hidden failure risk
- incompatibility with deterministic control

A pattern that looks powerful but undermines runtime discipline should not enter canon.
